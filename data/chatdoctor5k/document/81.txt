The dataset contains information about a disease called Hashimoto thyroiditis. The symptoms of this disease include weight gain, constipation, heavy menstrual flow, premenstrual tension or irritability, warts, difficulty in swallowing, too little hair, palpitations, heartburn, suprapubic pain, abnormal involuntary movements, and feeling hot. Medical tests that can be performed to diagnose the disease include hematologic tests (blood test), complete physical skin exam performed (ML), lipid panel, biopsy, ultrasonography (ultrasound), and hemoglobin A1c measurement (hemoglobin a1c test). The medications that can be used to treat Hashimoto thyroiditis include thyroxine (Synthroid), lithium (Li), sumatriptan (Imitrex), cannabis (Schedule I Substance), propylthiouracil, triiodothyronine (Cytomel), garlic preparation (Kwai), methimazole, and guanfacine (Intuniv).
