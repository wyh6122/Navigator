The csv describes a disease called Cryptorchidism and its associated symptoms, medical tests, and medications. Symptoms of the disease include swelling of the scrotum, pain in the testicles, flatulence, pus draining from the ear, jaundice, mass in the scrotum, lack of growth, white discharge from the eye, and an irritable infant. Medical tests that can be performed include a complete physical skin exam, ultrasonography, pelvis exam, rectal examination, and other procedures related to male genital and nervous system health, as well as occupational therapy assessment. Medications that can be used to treat the disease include Haemophilus B Conjugate Vaccine, Hepatitis B Vaccine, Rotavirus Vaccines, and Desoximetasone Topical.
