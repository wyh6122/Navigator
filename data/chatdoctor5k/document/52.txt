The csv describes a disease related to substance abuse and mental disorders. The symptoms include depressive or psychotic symptoms, delusions or hallucinations, depression, anxiety, drug abuse, alcohol abuse, insomnia, excessive anger, problems with movement, hostile behavior, fears and phobias. Medical tests required for diagnosis include hematologic tests, complete blood count, urinalysis, glucose measurement, kidney function tests, electrocardiogram, and intravenous fluid replacement. The medications prescribed include lorazepam, quetiapine, risperidone, haloperidol, benztropine, ziprasidone, efavirenz, naloxone, nicotine, didanosine, nelfinavir, and naltrexone.
