The csv describes a medical condition called Chalazion and its associated symptoms, medical tests and medications. Symptoms include a mass on the eyelid, pain in the eye, swollen eye, eye redness, eyelid swelling, eyelid lesion or rash, diminished vision, itchiness of the eye, skin growth, foreign body sensation in the eye, and lacrimation. Medical tests include excision (removal), other therapeutic procedures on eyelids, conjunctiva, cornea, ophthalmic examination and evaluation (eye exam), physical therapy exercises (exercises), physical therapy exercises, manipulation, and other procedures, and biopsy. Medications include dexamethasone-tobramycin ophthalmic, erythromycin, erythromycin ophthalmic, triamcinolone topical product, gentamicin ophthalmic, loteprednol-tobramycin ophthalmic, cefaclor (ceclor), tobramycin ophthalmic, fluorescein, ocular lubricant, ketorolac ophthalmic product, and ciprofloxacin ophthalmic.
