The given csv contains information about a disease called Von Hippel-Lindau disease. The symptoms of this disease include seizures, upper abdominal pain, excessive growth, abnormal appearing skin, headache, blood in urine, paresthesia, diminished vision, knee pain, fatigue, nausea, and kidney mass. The medical tests that can be done to diagnose this disease are magnetic resonance imaging (Mri), complete physical skin exam performed (ML), sigmoidoscopy or colonoscopy, incision and excision of CNS, diagnostic spinal tap (Spinal tap), excision of skin lesion, and occupational therapy assessment (Speech therapy). The medications that can be used to treat this disease include Albendazole, Pimecrolimus Topical, Acetazolamide (Diamox), Travoprost (Travatan), Bimatoprost Ophthalmic, Brimonidine Ophthalmic, Lactulose, Nortriptyline, Fluticasone Topical Product, Rabeprazole (Aciphex), Oxcarbazepine (Trileptal), and Gemfibrozil.
