The csv contains information about Nonalcoholic liver disease (NASH) and its related symptoms, medical tests, and medications. Symptoms include sharp abdominal pain, upper abdominal pain, nausea, dizziness, diarrhea, weight gain, mouth ulcer, chills, paresthesia, joint pain, nosebleed, and regurgitation. Medical tests for NASH include hematologic tests (blood test), complete blood count (CBC), glucose measurement (glucose level), lipid panel, electrolytes panel, X-ray computed tomography (scan CT), and intravenous fluid replacement. Medications for NASH include Vitamin E (Vita-E), Valganciclovir (Valcyte), Dapsone, Mycophenolate Mofetil (Cellcept), Lactulose, Cyclosporine, Nadolol, Mesalamine (Asacol), Epoetin Alfa (Procrit), Dutasteride (Avodart), Rabeprazole (Aciphex), and Glucosamine.
