The csv describes a disease called Subdural hemorrhage and its associated symptoms, medical tests, and medications. Symptoms of this disease include headache, dizziness, problems with movement, depressive or psychotic symptoms, seizures, vomiting, facial pain, disturbance of memory, irritable infant, abnormal movement of eyelid, hostile behavior, and delusions or hallucinations. Medical tests used to diagnose this disease include radiographic imaging procedures, X-ray computed tomography (Scan ct), hematologic tests (Blood test), complete blood count (Cbc), intravenous fluid replacement, plain x-ray (X ray), and CAT scan of head (Head ct). Medications used to treat this disease include Levetiracetam (Keppra), Phenytoin (Dilantin), Labetalol, Phenobarbital, Mannitol, Nicardipine, Vitamin K 1 (Mephyton), Carbidopa, Rocuronium, Linezolid (Zyvox), Dorzolamide Ophthalmic, and Fosphenytoin.
