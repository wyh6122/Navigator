Cornea infection can cause symptoms such as pain in the eye, redness, diminished vision, foreign body sensation, lacrimation, eye burns or stings, itchiness of the eye, spots or clouds in vision, swollen eye, and lip swelling. Medical tests such as ophthalmic examination and evaluation, therapeutic procedures on eyelids, conjunctiva and cornea, and ophthalmologic and otologic diagnosis and treatment may be performed. Medications that may be used include Moxifloxacin, Dexamethasone-Tobramycin Ophthalmic, Erythromycin, Proparacaine Ophthalmic, Prednisolone Ophthalmic, Loteprednol-Tobramycin Ophthalmic, Cyclopentolate Ophthalmic, Cyclosporine Ophthalmic, Fluorometholone Ophthalmic, Tetracaine, Tobramycin, and Ofloxacin Ophthalmic.
