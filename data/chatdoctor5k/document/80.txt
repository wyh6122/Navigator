The csv contains information about a disease called Alopecia. The symptoms of Alopecia include too little hair, irregular appearing scalp, skin rash, skin swelling, abnormal appearing skin, dry or flaky scalp, acne or pimples, skin dryness, peeling, scaliness, or roughness, itchy scalp, itching of skin, and feeling ill. Medical tests that can be performed to diagnose Alopecia include a complete physical skin exam, lipid panel, biopsy, excision (removal), and other diagnostic procedures on skin and subcutaneous tissue. Therapeutic procedures such as excision of skin lesion can also be performed. Medications that can be used to treat Alopecia include Minoxidil Topical, Finasteride (Propecia), Triamcinolone Topical Product, Clobetasol Topical, Fluocinonide Topical, Minoxidil (Rogaine), Betamethasone Topical Product, Salicylic Acid Topical, Ketoconazole, Hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil), Anthralin Topical, and Biotin.
