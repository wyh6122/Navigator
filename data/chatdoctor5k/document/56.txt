The csv describes a disease called Pituitary adenoma. Its symptoms include headache, loss of sensation, diminished vision, knee pain, nausea, disturbance of memory, coryza, weight gain, pain in the eye, nosebleed, long menstrual periods, and muscle swelling. Medical tests used to diagnose this disease include magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), examination of breast, other diagnostic procedures (interview; evaluation; consultation), pelvis exam, ophthalmic examination and evaluation (eye exam), diagnostic procedures on nose; mouth and pharynx, and sigmoidoscopy or colonoscopy. Medications used to treat this disease include bromocriptine, testosterone, rosuvastatin (Crestor), cabergoline, olmesartan (Benicar), gadoteridol (Prohance), pergolide, diethylpropion (Tenuate), pramlintide (Symlin), fluorometholone ophthalmic, and triazolam (Halcion).
