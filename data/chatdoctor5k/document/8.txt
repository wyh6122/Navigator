The csv contains information about a disease, its symptoms, medical tests to diagnose it and medications used to treat it. The disease is characterized by eye alignment disorder which may cause symptoms such as eye deviation, diminished vision, double vision, cross-eyed, pain in the eye, abnormal movement of eyelid, foreign body sensation in eye, lack of growth, irregular appearing scalp, and swollen lymph nodes. Medical tests used to diagnose this disease may include ophthalmologic and otologic diagnosis and treatment, other diagnostic procedures (interview, evaluation, consultation), and other therapeutic procedures on eyelids, conjunctiva, and cornea. Medications used to treat this disease may include Atropine (Uaa), Phenylephrine (Duramax), Cyclopentolate Ophthalmic, Tropicamide Ophthalmic, Dexamethasone-Tobramycin Ophthalmic, Methazolamide (Mzm), Fluorescein, Orlistat (Alli), Colloidal Oatmeal Topical, and Benzylpenicilloyl Polylysine (Pre-Pen).
