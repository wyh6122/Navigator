Pinworm infection can cause itching of the anus, sharp abdominal pain, pain of the anus, skin rash, constipation, itching of skin, painful urination, neck mass, retention of urine, ankle pain, and burning abdominal pain. To diagnose the disease, rectal examination, other diagnostic procedures on skin and subcutaneous tissue, ophthalmologic and otologic diagnosis and treatment, corneal transplant, procedures on spleen (Spleen operation), and removal of ectopic pregnancy are used. Some medications used to treat the disease include Mebendazole, Pyrantel (Evict), Magaldrate, Lactulose, Clotrimazole Topical, Mecamylamine, Grepafloxacin (Raxar), Malathion Topical, Gemtuzumab (Mylotarg), and Gadoteridol (Prohance).
